The videogame industry is bigger than movies and american sports combined, thanks to covid.

October 2, 2022 Author Ikobi Lynch

Global videogame revenue is expected to surge 20% to 179.7 billion in 2020, according to IDC data. The global film industry reached $100 billion in revenue for the first time in 2019, and PwC estimated North American sports would bring in more than $75 billion in 2020. Both industried suffered from the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020. With the release of next generation consoled from Sony Corp strong growth in the industry has continued to be strong.

Micro-Transactions are killing user experience but developers continue to see excellent profits.

October 2, 2022 Author: Ikobi Lynch

What started off as a convienient way to unlock a new skin in a game without spending countless hours grinding, has now become the straw that broke the camels back. Gamers are outraged at how microtransactions has been implemented in recent games from the big publishers EA, Activision, Blizzard etc... Gamers has continuously seen the successors of their favourite game series turn into a pay to win free for all where you no longer need to actually be good at the game, if you have enough money just pay for the best weapons, the best powerups and easily cruise your way to victory while the broke haters just can't admit how much better you are than them.
